Frequently Asked Questions

1
What are the common symptoms of eye diseases?
▼
Common symptoms of eye diseases include blurred vision, difficulty seeing at night, eye pain or discomfort, redness, itching, watering of the eyes, and seeing halos around lights.
2
How are eye diseases diagnosed?
▼
Eye diseases can be diagnosed through a comprehensive eye examination, including visual acuity tests, dilated eye exams, tonometry (eye pressure measurement), and imaging tests such as optical coherence tomography (OCT) or fundus photography.
3
What are the treatment options for common eye diseases?
▼
Treatment options for common eye diseases may include prescription eyeglasses or contact lenses, medication (such as eye drops), laser therapy, surgery (such as cataract surgery or corneal transplant), or lifestyle changes.
4
Are all eye diseases preventable?
▼
While some eye diseases may have genetic or unavoidable risk factors, many can be prevented or their progression slowed through regular eye exams, wearing protective eyewear, maintaining a healthy lifestyle, and avoiding habits such as smoking.
5
Can eye diseases lead to permanent vision loss?
▼
Yes, some eye diseases, if left untreated or poorly managed, can lead to permanent vision loss. It's important to seek prompt medical attention and follow recommended treatment plans to preserve vision.
